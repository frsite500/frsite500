About Me

I was born in Spain at the time of the Civil War, spent my early years in Vichy France, received my primary education in Mexico and my secondary school and university education in New Zealand where I graduated in medicine.

I returned to Europe and settled in London, where I obtained my London Fellowship in Anaesthesia and was appointed Senior Lecturer and Consultant at the Royal London Hospital.

Concurrent with my hospital work and since retiring, I have translated literary works from Spanish into English and launched into my own writing. My autobiography, My Innocent Absence, Arcadia Books, was published in 2010.

I am married and have two daughters.
